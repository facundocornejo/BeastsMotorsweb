# Beast Motors — Design Context

> This file is the single source of truth for design decisions across all skills.
> Updated: 2026-04-14

---

## Users

**Two distinct segments sharing one interface:**

1. **Budget buyers (18-35):** Lower-to-middle income, price-sensitive, browsing used economy vehicles ($3k-$15k). Tech-comfortable but not sophisticated. Browsing on cheap Android phones over 4G. May be casual browsers or impulse-driven.

2. **Premium buyers (35-55+):** Upper-middle to high income, actively shopping used SUVs/trucks or 0km imports ($25k-$45k+). Deliberate decision-makers. Expect a polished, trustworthy experience. Browsing on iPhones and desktops.

**Shared behavior:** WhatsApp-first communication (culturally normalized in Argentina). Low attention span for browsing — vehicle photos drive engagement, not text. Stock is tiny (3-14 vehicles), so the interface must feel full and alive even with minimal inventory.

**Context of use:** Primarily mobile (80%+), often at night or during downtime. Dark theme aligns with evening browsing patterns.

---

## Brand Personality

**Three words:** Modern, Aggressive, Dynamic

**Voice:** Confident and direct. Not luxury-pretentious, not budget-cheap. A dealership that takes itself seriously but doesn't take itself too seriously. Speaks like a knowledgeable friend who happens to sell cars — professional but approachable.

**Emotional goals (in order):**
1. **Trust & legitimacy** — "This is a real, established business"
2. **Professional solidity** — "I can rely on these people"
3. **Brand identity** — The color palette IS the brand. Logo colors are non-negotiable DNA.

**What Beast Motors is NOT:**
- Not a tech startup (no SaaS aesthetics)
- Not a marketplace (no MercadoLibre/Kavak vibes)
- Not a luxury boutique (no cold minimalism)
- Not a used-car lot with cheap vibes

## Aesthetic Direction

### Theme: Nighttime Showroom

Dark-first. Not "dark mode as afterthought" — dark is the primary, intentional experience. Think: walking into a well-lit showroom at night. The vehicles are the stars, spotlit against dark surfaces. High contrast. Bold color. The darkness isn't moody or techy — it's theatrical.

Light mode exists only for admin panel. Public site = dark.

### Color Palette (LOCKED — derived from logo, non-negotiable)

| Role | Token | Hex | Notes |
|------|-------|-----|-------|
| CTA / Accent | `--rose` | `#D91E50` | Primary action color. WhatsApp buttons, price highlights, badges |
| Rose hover | `--rose-dark` | `#B8183F` | Hover/pressed states |
| Primary brand | `--blue-deep` | `#03588C` | Headers, primary containers |
| Secondary | `--blue-mid` | `#0578A6` | Secondary elements, links |
| Highlight | `--blue-light` | `#24A3BF` | Hover states, active indicators |
| Warm neutral | `--cream` | `#F2DCC2` | Accent surfaces, warm highlights |
| WhatsApp | `--green-wa` | `#25D366` | WhatsApp indicator only |

**Dark theme surfaces:** Deep teal-blacks (current `#051313` base). Push darker and more saturated than V5A prototype. Client wants MORE color, MORE boldness, MORE visual presence.

**Neutrals:** Tint toward the blue-deep hue, not pure gray. Even at `0.005` chroma in OKLCH, the teal tint creates subconscious brand cohesion.

### Typography (OPEN — client rejected previous fonts)

**Previous fonts REJECTED:** Plus Jakarta Sans + Fraunces. Client wants "modern, aggressive, dynamic."

**Direction:** The typography must convey power and modernity. NOT elegant-serif + friendly-geometric (that was the old direction). Think: confident, slightly bold, with character.

**Selection criteria:**
- Display font: Should feel powerful, not decorative. Wide or condensed proportions. Strong presence at large sizes.
- Body font: Clean, highly readable at small sizes on mobile. Neutral but not generic.
- Must work well in Spanish (accents: á, é, í, ó, ú, ñ, ü)

**BANNED fonts (reflex defaults — do not use):**
Inter, DM Sans, Plus Jakarta Sans, Outfit, Instrument Sans, Space Grotesk, Fraunces, Playfair Display, Cormorant, Lora, Crimson Pro, Syne, IBM Plex (any), Space Mono, DM Serif Display, Instrument Serif, Poppins, Roboto, Open Sans, Arial.

**Exploration directions:**
- Display: Look at Clash Display, General Sans, Satoshi, Switzer, Cabinet Grotesk (Fontshare); or Neue Montreal, Neue Machina, Monument Extended (Pangram Pangram); or Manrope, Lexend, Albert Sans, Geist (Google/system).
- Body: Pair with a clean geometric or humanist sans. Consider the display font's companion family if one exists.
- Monospace accent (for prices/stats): Optional. If used, pick something with character.

### Visual Elements (LOCKED)

- **WhatsApp button:** Rectangular, floating, persistent. Always accessible.
- **Logo:** Used as icon/favicon only. Brand name displayed as styled text (not logo image).
- **Cards:** Rounded corners (`border-radius: 10px`), subtle shadows, hover lift (`translateY(-3px)`).
- **Sort dropdown:** Inspired by carone.com.ar aesthetic.
- **"Recien ingresados" carousel:** High visual prominence, separate from main catalog grid.
- **"Ventas Felices" gallery:** Sold vehicles + customer photos. No dates shown.

### Layout Philosophy

- Mobile-first is non-negotiable (80%+ mobile traffic)
- Small inventory (3-14 vehicles) means the layout must feel alive with few items
- Photos are the primary content — let them breathe, make them large
- Asymmetry and varied card sizes can make small inventory feel more dynamic
- Left-aligned text preferred over centered-everything

### Motion

- Staggered reveal on page load for vehicle cards
- Smooth carousel transitions for "Recien ingresados"
- Hover lifts on cards (transform only, no layout shifts)
- Keep it purposeful — this is a catalog, not a portfolio site

## Design Principles

1. **Vehicles are the hero.** Every design decision should make the cars look their best. Large photos, high contrast, minimal UI chrome competing for attention.

2. **Small stock, big presence.** With only 3-14 vehicles, the interface must never feel empty. Use layout variation, featured sections, and visual weight to make minimal inventory feel curated rather than sparse.

3. **Dark is deliberate, not default.** The dark theme isn't "dark mode" — it's a showroom. Surfaces recede, vehicles and CTAs pop. Every bright element earns its brightness.

4. **WhatsApp is the finish line.** Every scroll, every click, every interaction should reduce the distance to a WhatsApp tap. The CTA should feel inevitable, not pushy.

5. **Trust through craft.** The target audience includes people spending $45k+. The interface must feel as solid as that investment. No jank, no broken states, no cheap shortcuts.

## Anti-Patterns (Explicit Bans)

- No MercadoLibre-style dense grids with tiny text
- No Kavak-style sterile white marketplace aesthetic
- No generic SaaS dashboards with purple gradients
- No glassmorphism or blur-heavy decorative effects
- No gradient text
- No colored left-border accent stripes on cards
- No bounce/elastic animations
- No light theme for public-facing pages

---

## Accessibility

- WCAG 2.1 AA minimum
- Touch targets: 44x44px minimum
- Contrast ratios must meet AA on dark backgrounds (critical given dark theme)
- Support for reduced-motion preferences
- Semantic HTML, proper heading hierarchy
- No accessibility requirements beyond standard compliance

## Technical Constraints

- Next.js 16 (App Router, RSC), TypeScript, Tailwind CSS v4
- No Vercel-specific features (migrating to Oracle Cloud in ~3 months)
- Cloudinary for all images (non-negotiable)
- Supabase for auth + data
- Performance: <2s load on mobile 4G, Lighthouse 85+ mobile
- Browser support: iOS Safari 14+, Chrome/Edge latest 2, Samsung Internet
